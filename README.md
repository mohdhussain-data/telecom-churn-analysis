## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes customer churn data from a telecom company to identify why customers leave, which segments are at highest risk, and what actions can reduce churn. With a 26.54% churn rate and $139K in monthly revenue lost, this analysis addresses a critical business problem.

---

## 🎯 Business Questions Answered
- What is the overall churn rate and revenue impact?
- Which contract types have the highest churn?
- When do customers typically churn (tenure analysis)?
- Which payment methods correlate with churn?
- Do demographics (age, gender, family status) affect churn?
- Which services reduce churn risk?
- Are high-paying customers more likely to leave?

## 🔍 Key Findings

1. **26.54% Churn Rate:** Over 1 in 4 customers is leaving — critical for business sustainability
2. **Contract Type is #1 Driver:** Month-to-month customers churn at 42.71% vs 2.83% for two-year contracts
3. **First Year is Critical:** 47.44% of churned customers leave within the first 12 months
4. **Electronic Check is High-Risk:** 45.29% churn rate vs 15.24% for auto-pay credit card
5. **Senior Citizens at Risk:** 41.68% churn rate vs 23.61% for non-seniors
6. **Security Services Retain Customers:** Customers without OnlineSecurity churn at 41.77% vs 14.61% with it
7. **High Charges Drive Churn:** Churned customers pay ~$74/month vs ~$61 for retained customers

---

## 💡 Recommendations

1. **Convert month-to-month customers to annual contracts** with discount incentives
2. **Implement 90-day onboarding program** — first year retention is critical
3. **Incentivize auto-pay adoption** — reduces churn by 30 percentage points
4. **Upsell OnlineSecurity and TechSupport** during onboarding
5. **Create senior citizen retention program** with simplified billing
6. **Review pricing for high-charge customers** — offer loyalty discounts above $70/month
